## Topics Discussed

- We kicked off the meeting with introductions and a quick chat about the challenges of Monday morning syncs.
- The group reviewed the charter's core mission to build a shared taxonomy standard and a high-level ecosystem directory.
- We explored the end goals of eliminating siloed terminology and preventing duplicate research across working groups.
- Junjie presented the new decoupled website and data structure that enables a PR-driven, asynchronous contribution workflow.
- The team discussed leveraging industry-standard CNCF-style schemas for our taxonomy data instead of reinventing the wheel.
- Pavan suggested ensuring taxonomy definitions cater to both beginners and experts by including concise summaries alongside detailed scope notes.
- We clarified the distinction between member-driven and external entities within the landscape map structure.
- The group discussed the crucial role of domain editors and working group delegates in reviewing and approving cross-cutting terminology.
- We aligned on the strategy to merge our landscape directory efforts with the existing landscape.aif.io project.

## Decisions

- We will adopt a PR-driven, asynchronous workflow for all taxonomy contributions to maximize efficiency.
- We will utilize the CNCF-style metadata structure as the baseline schema for our shared vocabulary.
- We will structure our taxonomy definitions to include both a quick summary and a comprehensive description field to resolve ambiguity.
- We decided to integrate our landscape map with the existing landscape.aif.io while exploring a dedicated domain for the taxonomy.
